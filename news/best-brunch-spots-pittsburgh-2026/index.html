Best Brunch Spots in Pittsburgh

The 12 best brunch spots in Pittsburgh for 2026. Weekend favorites from upscale dining to beloved neighborhood diners across the city.

By The Pittsburgh Wire May 5, 2026 6 min read

Pittsburgh takes its weekend brunch seriously. From elegant dining rooms serving champagne-paired French pastries to no-frills diners flipping the city's most famous hotcakes, the brunch scene offers something for every mood and budget. Whether you want bottomless mimosas and Eggs Benedict or a massive stack of pancakes at a cash-only counter, these twelve spots deliver the best brunch experiences in Pittsburgh.

1. The Nook

South Side • 275 E Carson St, Pittsburgh, PA 15203

An extensive brunch menu with Eggs Benedict variations, avocado toast, and Green Thing pancakes with pistachio paste and banana. Creative cocktails including specialty Bloody Marys.

2. Pamela's P&G Diner

Strip District • 60 21st St, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

A Pittsburgh institution famous for thin, crispy-edged hotcakes that earned a presidential shoutout. Cash only, expect a line, absolutely worth it.

3. Paris 66

East Liberty • 6018 Centre Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

Authentic French brunch with champagne, croissants, macarons, and pastries from Gaby et Jules bakery. Feels like a Parisian cafe.

4. Ritual House

Downtown • 501 Grant St, Pittsburgh, PA 15219

Creative brunch in the stunning Union Trust Building. Fruity Pebble French Toast and decadent pancake options make this brunch as spectacle.

5. DiAnoia's Eatery

Strip District • 2549 Penn Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

House-made pastries, frittatas with seasonal ingredients, and Italian-inflected twists on brunch classics. World-class espresso drinks.

6. The Speckled Egg PGH

Downtown • 301 Fourth Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

Creative egg dishes, excellent coffee, and a bright modern space. Reliable for both weekday breakfasts and leisurely weekend brunches.

7. Grand Concourse

Station Square • 1 Station Square, Pittsburgh, PA 15219

Lavish Sunday brunch buffet in a magnificent restored 1901 railroad station beneath soaring stained-glass ceilings with riverfront views.

8. Lola's Eatery

Lawrenceville • 5765 Baum Blvd, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

Filipino-inspired brunch with standout ube cinnamon rolls, longanisa breakfast plates, and creative fusion dishes. Lines every weekend.

9. The Zenith

South Side • 86 S 26th St, Pittsburgh, PA 15203

Part vegetarian restaurant, part antique store. Weekend brunch surrounded by vintage furnishings and art, all for sale. Creative and always surprising.

10. Cafe Moulin

Shadyside • 227 N Highland Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

Charming French cafe serving authentic crepes, fresh pastries, quiches, and excellent coffee. Feels like a neighborhood secret.

11. Tupelo Honey

Downtown • 350 Oliver Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

Southern-inspired brunch with scratch-made biscuits, shrimp and grits, chicken and waffles, and boozy cocktails. Generous portions and warm hospitality.

12. Butterwood Bake Consortium

Lawrenceville • 4003 Butler St, Pittsburgh, PA 15201

Extraordinary scones, croissants, and seasonal pastries baked fresh daily. The line moves fast and everything is worth it.
