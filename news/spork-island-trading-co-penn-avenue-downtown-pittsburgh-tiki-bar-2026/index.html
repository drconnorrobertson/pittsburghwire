Pittsburgh's Boldest New Bar Brings Erupting Volcanoes to Penn Avenue

Spork Island Trading Co. opens this summer at 946 Penn Ave, delivering an immersive tiki world of cascading waterfalls, chef-driven yakitori, and a rum program built to rival any tropical port city.

The Pittsburgh Wire Editorial Staff
July 21, 2026
4 Min Read
SPORK ISLAND
Spork Island Trading Co. at 946 Penn Avenue brings an immersive tropical world to downtown Pittsburgh. | Rendering courtesy of Spork Island Trading Company

Pittsburgh has never lacked for personality, but the city's Penn Avenue corridor is about to add something it has never seen before: a full-scale tropical escape, complete with an erupting volcano, cascading waterfalls, a hidden cave, and winding jungle pathways — all tucked behind a downtown door at 946 Penn Avenue.

Spork Island Trading Company is the brainchild of two of Pittsburgh's most seasoned hospitality minds: Andy Tepper, founder of the beloved Spork restaurant that helped define the city's modern dining scene, and Steve Zumoff, the man behind a string of South Side institutions including the Tiki Lounge, Lava Lounge, and the Beehive. Together, they have spent years quietly building what may be the most ambitious bar concept in Pittsburgh's recent history.

The concept is deceptively simple in its premise: step through the front door and leave Pittsburgh behind. What greets guests is a fully realized tropical world — binchotan-fired yakitori sending up thin plumes of fragrant smoke, lantern-lit tiki huts for private parties, booths tucked into jungle alcoves, and a hidden cave that has already generated significant buzz on social media before the first drink has been poured to the public. The interior was designed in collaboration with renowned tiki designer Bamboo Ben, whose work has appeared in celebrated venues across the country, and the result is something closer to theater than restaurant.

"Step through the door and into a tropical world of erupting volcano displays, cascading waterfalls, a hidden cave, and winding jungle pathways — a setting unlike anything else in the city."
Spork Island Trading Co., on its Pittsburgh debut

The food program sets Spork Island apart from the novelty-focused tiki bars of a previous era. The kitchen is chef-driven, centering on yakitori skewers grilled over traditional Japanese binchotan charcoal — a technique that produces remarkably clean, even heat and a faint woodsy char that elevates even a simple chicken thigh into something worth savoring. Shareable plates round out the menu with globally inspired flavors that, the team promises, are built with the same precision and intention that has guided their other ventures.

Behind the bar, an extensive rum collection anchors a tropical cocktail program made with fresh-squeezed juices, house-made syrups, and premium spirits. The menu moves fluidly between island classics executed with technical rigor and original creations that play with unexpected flavor combinations. It is, by design, the kind of bar where a first visit produces a list of things to order next time.

A Long Road to Penn Avenue

The path to opening was not without its detours. Tepper and Zumoff originally envisioned Spork Island for a South Side location at the former Double Wide Grill on East Carson Street, proposing an exterior design that included towering moai figures, tropical trellis work, and, most memorably, a corner volcano. The city's Historic Review Commission rejected that exterior plan in December 2023, citing incompatibility with the East Carson Street historic district guidelines. It was a setback, but not a defeat.

Rather than water down the vision, the partners redirected it. The Penn Avenue location, free from the exterior constraints of a historic review district, gave them the canvas they needed. The interior now houses everything the team originally imagined and more, without compromise. In a city with a long memory for what gets built and what gets stalled, Spork Island is a case study in creative persistence — the kind of story Pittsburgh has told about itself since the days of the steel mills.

What It Means for Downtown

Spork Island's arrival on Penn Avenue comes at a meaningful moment for downtown Pittsburgh. The corridor has been in the midst of a sustained dining and entertainment revival, with new concepts from veteran operators helping anchor foot traffic that strengthens the case for further investment. A destination venue of this caliber — one that draws guests from across the region specifically for the experience — adds exactly the kind of gravitational pull that neighborhood advocates and the Pittsburgh Downtown Partnership have been working toward.

It also arrives as Pittsburgh's restaurant scene is receiving unprecedented national attention. The Michelin Guide is expected to evaluate the region in 2026, and this summer alone has seen more than fifty new restaurants, bars, and cafes open or announce imminent openings. That volume of activity reflects genuine confidence in Pittsburgh's dining public — their adventurousness, their loyalty to well-made things, and their appetite for places that feel like nowhere else.

Spork Island Trading Co. fits that description exactly. In a city that has always found ways to be surprising, it manages to feel both entirely of this place and completely unlike anything that has come before it. That is no small feat, and Pittsburgh's Penn Avenue is better for it.

Spork Island Trading Co. is located at 946 Penn Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15222, and is open Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday from 4 p.m. to 11 p.m. Kitchen hours run 4:30 p.m. to 11 p.m. Follow updates at @sporkisland on Instagram.
