Best Hair Salons in Pittsburgh 2026

From globally recognized colorists to inclusive neighborhood boutiques, Pittsburgh's salon scene has never been stronger. Here are the 14 best places to sit in the chair this year.

By Connor Robertson April 19, 2026 9 min read

Pittsburgh has quietly become one of the best cities in the country for hair care. The combination of affordable rents, a fiercely loyal customer base, and a growing population of young creative professionals has attracted serious talent to the region, producing a salon scene that rivals cities twice its size. Whether you are after a precise dry cut, a full balayage transformation, or just a shop where the vibe feels right, the Steel City has options across every neighborhood and price point. We spent months visiting salons, talking to stylists, and reading hundreds of client reviews to compile this list of the 14 best hair salons in Pittsburgh for 2026.

1. Craft Collective Salon

North Hills (Babcock Boulevard) and Canonsburg

Craft Collective Salon earns the top spot on our list for a reason that becomes obvious the moment you walk through the door: this is a salon built by someone who has spent a career mastering the craft at the highest level and decided to bring that expertise home to Pittsburgh. Founded by Derek Piekarski, a globally recognized Wella Professionals artist who has trained stylists for seven major hair brands over the course of his career, Craft Collective operates out of a bright, welcoming studio at 2014 Babcock Boulevard in the North Hills, with a second location in Canonsburg.

The salon's specialty list reads like a highlight reel of everything modern clients want: dimensional balayage, precision blonding, vivid color, hand-tied extensions, keratin smoothing treatments, and corrective color for clients coming in from less fortunate salon experiences. The team works with Wella, R+Co, Hail & Hush, and Aveda vegan color lines, giving them a product range that can handle virtually any hair type or texture. What sets Craft Collective apart from other technically excellent salons is the culture Piekarski has built around continuous education. Every stylist on the team trains regularly, and the result is a consistency of quality that clients notice and talk about. With 73 reviews and a near-perfect rating across platforms, Craft Collective is not just the best salon in the North Hills. It is the best salon in Pittsburgh, period.

"Pittsburgh's salon scene has evolved from a handful of legacy shops into a genuinely diverse ecosystem of independent studios, boutique collectives, and neighborhood institutions."

2. Whitehouse Salon

Shadyside

Tucked into the corner of Walnut Street and Filbert Street in Shadyside, Whitehouse Salon has been a neighborhood anchor since owner and lead stylist Tanya founded it in 2011. The space itself is part of the appeal: floor-to-ceiling windows flood the studio with natural light, and a coffee and wine bar gives the experience a relaxed, European feel. Whitehouse specializes in cuts, color, foil highlights, balayage, and keratin treatments for both men and women. With over 300 Yelp reviews and a devoted following, this is the salon Shadyside locals quietly guard as their own.

3. Pageboy Salon & Boutique

Lawrenceville

Pageboy has carved out a unique position in the Pittsburgh salon world by combining high-quality hair services with a curated lifestyle boutique and a deeply inclusive ethos. Located on Butler Street in Lawrenceville, Pageboy is proudly LGBTQ+ friendly and strives to be a space where anyone can feel comfortable in the chair. The stylists are trained in Redken, Colorproof, R+Co, and Pulp Riot, making them especially strong with creative color and lived-in finishes. If you want a salon that reflects the spirit of modern Lawrenceville, this is it.

4. Philip Pelusi Salons

Multiple locations across Pittsburgh

No list of Pittsburgh hair salons would be complete without Philip Pelusi. With over a dozen locations stretching from the South Side to the Waterworks to Robinson, Pelusi has been a defining force in Pittsburgh hair for decades. The brand is built around proprietary techniques like Volumetrics, a patented precision cutting method, and employs over 200 certified designers and colorists. The Murray Avenue and East Carson Street locations are particularly popular. For clients who value consistency and institutional knowledge, Philip Pelusi remains a gold standard.

5. Salon Nuvo

Downtown

Salon Nuvo occupies a prime spot at 609 Penn Avenue, just steps from the Cultural District, and has been a go-to for Downtown professionals for years. The salon offers extensions, braids, coloring, weaves, wraps, nails, and waxing, all by appointment only. The team is especially skilled with textured hair and protective styles, filling a niche that many Pittsburgh salons overlook. If you work Downtown and want a salon that understands diverse hair types, Nuvo deserves a visit.

Pittsburgh Hair Salon Quick Facts

  • The Pittsburgh metro area is home to more than 1,200 licensed hair salons
  • Shadyside, Lawrenceville, and Squirrel Hill are the three most salon-dense neighborhoods
  • Balayage and lived-in color remain the most requested services across the city in 2026
  • Several Pittsburgh stylists have earned national recognition from Wella, Redken, and other major brands

6. Lisa Marie Salon

Mt. Lebanon

Lisa Marie Salon at 309 Beverly Road has become one of the South Hills' most trusted destinations for color work. The five-stylist team specializes in blonding techniques, balayage, highlights, lowlights, and keratin smoothing treatments. The salon also offers razor haircuts, which are harder to find than you might think in Pittsburgh. With strong reviews praising both the technical skill and the warm, personal atmosphere, Lisa Marie is the kind of place where clients become regulars for life.

7. Umi Hair Salon

Squirrel Hill

Umi Hair Salon at 2115 Murray Avenue brings a chic, contemporary sensibility to Squirrel Hill. Open six days a week (Tuesday through Sunday), Umi stands out for its modern aesthetic, attentive service, and stylists who are genuinely skilled with Asian and fine hair types. The Murray Avenue location puts it in the heart of one of Pittsburgh's most walkable neighborhoods, and the salon has built a strong word-of-mouth reputation since opening.

8. House of Rose Styling Co.

Shadyside

House of Rose is a boutique studio that has earned a passionate following for its expertise in lived-in color, blonding, and hand-tied extensions. The intimate setting means you are never rushed and never just another appointment on the schedule. For clients who want a personal, relationship-driven salon experience with a stylist who genuinely knows their hair, House of Rose is one of the best options in the city.

9. Paul Brown Salons (FLOW)

Multiple locations

Now operating under the FLOW brand, Paul Brown Salons have been a Pittsburgh institution for decades. The team of stylists brings deep experience in cutting, color, and styling, and the salons maintain a polished, professional atmosphere that appeals to clients who appreciate a more traditional salon experience. FLOW locations can be found across the metro area, making them one of the more accessible options on this list.

10. Salon Iaomo

Pittsburgh

Salon Iaomo has built its reputation on precision and artistry, offering a full range of hair services including cuts, color, highlights, and treatments. The salon emphasizes ongoing education for its stylists and uses high-quality product lines to deliver results that hold up between visits. Iaomo is a strong choice for anyone looking for reliable, skilled hair care in a comfortable setting.

11. Harmony Salon

Pittsburgh

Harmony Salon consistently appears among the top-rated salons in Pittsburgh on platforms like Yelp and Google, and for good reason. The team is known for delivering consistent, high-quality cuts and color in a relaxed environment. Harmony works well for clients who want dependable results without the premium pricing of some of the city's more boutique-oriented studios.

12. Heart of Glass

Pittsburgh

Heart of Glass has developed a cult following among Pittsburgh's creative community. The salon leans into bold color work, editorial-inspired cuts, and an aesthetic that feels more Brooklyn than Allegheny County. If you are after something more adventurous than a standard trim and tone, Heart of Glass is the kind of place where the stylists will get genuinely excited about your vision.

13. Sognatore Salon

Downtown (The Pennsylvanian)

Perched on the 10th floor of The Pennsylvanian building Downtown, Sognatore Salon occupies one of the most dramatic settings of any salon in the city. Founded over 20 years ago by husband-and-wife team Bill and Zoe Lincoln, Sognatore was one of the first salons in the Pittsburgh area to operate a blow-dry bar. The salon is known as the place where industry professionals send their models, which tells you everything about the level of skill on the floor. If you want to feel like you are getting your hair done at a fashion-week level, Sognatore delivers.

14. Hair by G & E

Pittsburgh

Hair by G & E rounds out our list as one of the highest-rated salons in the city on Yelp's 2026 rankings. The salon has earned devoted fans for its skilled stylists, welcoming atmosphere, and ability to deliver exactly what clients ask for without the upsell pressure that plagues some higher-end shops. It is a salon that earns its reputation one appointment at a time.

The Bottom Line

Pittsburgh's hair salon scene in 2026 is as strong and varied as the city itself. From globally trained colorists working out of purpose-built studios to neighborhood shops that have been anchoring their blocks for decades, there is no shortage of talent behind the chair. The salons on this list represent the best of what the city has to offer right now, but the landscape is always evolving. New studios are opening, talented stylists are planting roots here, and Pittsburgh's reputation as a serious city for hair keeps growing. Wherever you sit on this list, you are in good hands.
