The Esplanade Breaks Ground: $740M North Side Development Takes Shape

Piatt Companies breaks ground on a transformative mixed-use development featuring seven buildings with residential, retail, office space, and an iconic Ferris wheel, beginning 12 months of intensive construction.

By Tony MarchettiApril 19, 20266 min read

The North Shore riverfront continues to evolve with The Esplanade, connecting Pittsburgh's entertainment district to major new mixed-use development. // Photo: Unsplash

Pittsburgh's North Side received its most significant development catalyst in years as Piatt Companies officially broke ground on The Esplanade on December 1, 2025, initiating a major mixed-use transformation that will reshape the neighborhood's physical character and economic function. The $740 million project comprises seven buildings incorporating residential units, retail space, office environments, and a distinctive Ferris wheel landmark. Twelve months of active site work lie ahead as contractors mobilize equipment, commence demolition of existing structures, and begin foundation construction for the development's building components.

The Esplanade represents the largest single development project on Pittsburgh's North Side in over a decade. The scale and scope fundamentally alter the neighborhood's identity from an industrial and automotive service district toward a vibrant mixed-use urban center. The inclusion of both residential and commercial space creates opportunities for residents to live, work, dine, and conduct leisure activities within walking distance. This pedestrian-oriented development model contrasts sharply with the automobile-dependent sprawl that historically characterized North Side land use patterns.

The Ferris wheel component serves as both a transportation mechanism and iconic architectural landmark. Rather than functioning as a utilitarian structure, the Ferris wheel becomes a destination attraction drawing visitors and creating a distinctive Pittsburgh identity marker visible across the region. Viewing platforms provide panoramic perspectives of Pittsburgh's urban landscape, the Three Rivers system, and surrounding neighborhoods. This tourist amenity generates sustained foot traffic that benefits retail tenants, restaurants, and hospitality operations throughout the development.

"The Esplanade will fundamentally reposition the North Side as a destination rather than a pass-through neighborhood, generating economic activity that benefits the entire region."

Piatt Companies engaged Mascaro Construction as the primary contractor managing day-to-day site operations and coordinating subcontractors across multiple trades. Mascaro's experience with large-scale Pittsburgh development projects positions the firm to navigate the complex logistics of constructing seven buildings simultaneously while maintaining safety protocols and quality standards. The contractor's local expertise becomes particularly valuable given the project's proximity to existing neighborhoods, where construction impacts require careful management and community coordination.

The residential component addresses Pittsburgh's growing housing demand as the city attracts talent from technology, healthcare, and professional service sectors. The Esplanade's location on the North Side positions residents near employment centers in Downtown Pittsburgh while offering waterfront proximity increasingly valued by urban professionals. The development's retail and dining offerings create neighborhood amenities that support residential viability and reduce residents' need to travel for daily services.

Office space within The Esplanade targets technology and professional service companies seeking modern, efficient work environments. Pittsburgh's maturing tech ecosystem generates demand for office space that accommodates the flexible work requirements of software companies, consulting firms, and digital service providers. The Esplanade's mixed-use positioning allows office tenants to benefit from retail dining, hospitality, and entertainment amenities adjacent to their workplaces, creating competitive advantages relative to traditional office parks or downtown tower locations.

The development's significance extends beyond the immediate project scope to broader North Side neighborhood trajectory. Successful completion of The Esplanade will catalyze secondary development as adjacent property owners recognize changed market conditions and neighborhood positioning. Infrastructure improvements, streetscape enhancements, and transit access investments will ripple outward, generating complementary developments that amplify the primary project's economic impact. Pittsburgh's North Side stands positioned to transition from declining industrial neighborhood to vibrant mixed-use urban district, representing a transformative moment in the city's ongoing physical and economic evolution.
