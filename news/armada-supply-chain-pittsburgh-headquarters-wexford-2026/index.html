Armada Opens 90,000-Square-Foot Headquarters in Wexford, Tripling Its Pittsburgh Footprint

One of Pittsburgh's largest and oldest privately held companies has staked a bold claim on the region's future, moving 300 employees into a state-of-the-art campus designed for the next century of growth.

By The Pittsburgh Wire Staff May 20, 2026 4 min read
ARMADA

Armada's new three-story headquarters at Innovation Ridge, Wexford, opened Tuesday, May 19, 2026. Photo: Armada Supply Chain Solutions

Pittsburgh has always been a city that builds things. For more than 135 years, Armada has been no different. On Tuesday, May 19, the supply chain giant cut the ribbon on its new headquarters at Innovation Ridge in Wexford — a gleaming, 90,000-square-foot campus that represents the company's most ambitious investment in the Pittsburgh region since its founding as a North Side meatpacker in 1890.

The move triples Armada's previous office footprint. The company had been operating out of roughly 15,000 to 20,000 square feet in Fox Chapel, a space that could no longer contain its ambitions. Now headquartered at 2000 Innovation Drive within the RIDC Innovation Ridge business park, Armada is initially occupying 65,000 square feet across three stories, with the remaining space held in reserve for the growth its leadership fully expects to come.

Armada by the Numbers
$5.8B Revenue generated in 2025, ranking Armada among Pittsburgh's top three largest privately held companies
300+ Employees based at the new Wexford headquarters, returning to a hybrid schedule of three in-office days per week
90,000 Square feet of new office space at Innovation Ridge, a sixfold increase over the previous Fox Chapel location
136 Years of continuous Pittsburgh history, from North Side meatpacker in 1890 to global supply chain orchestrator today

More than 300 Armada employees began reporting to the new campus this week under a hybrid schedule: three days in the office, two days remote. It marks a significant cultural shift for a company that had largely gone remote after the pandemic, with many workers coming in just five days a month. The new headquarters is designed to make that return feel less like a mandate and more like a reward.

"Connecting the dots is what we do. We are right in the middle of a supply chain, and that takes a lot of collaboration."

Chris O'Brien, CEO, Armada Supply Chain Solutions

The building's interior reflects that philosophy at every turn. Twenty-six conference rooms are distributed throughout the floors. Private phone and video booths offer quiet space for focused work. A massive training center anchors the first floor. Six treadmill desks line a sun-drenched window wall overlooking preserved wetlands and woodlands. Corner offices — typically reserved for executive suites in a previous corporate era — have been deliberately kept as shared conference rooms, giving the best views to collaboration rather than hierarchy.

A Company Built for the Next Chapter

Armada is more than a logistics company. It is Pittsburgh's third-largest privately held enterprise, trailing only Giant Eagle and 84 Lumber, and generated approximately $5.8 billion in revenue last year according to Forbes. In 2025, the company was named McDonald's U.S. Supplier of the Year, a recognition that underscores its central role in the food service supply chain across North America and beyond.

CEO Chris O'Brien sees the Wexford move as both a consolidation of that existing strength and a launch pad for expansion into new sectors. He has outlined plans to push Armada deeper into general manufacturing, the automotive industry, and Pittsburgh's fast-growing life science, health care, and robotics sectors. The Innovation Ridge location, with its proximity to key transportation corridors and the regional talent pipeline, is positioned to support that ambition.

NAI Burns Scalo served as general contractor for the project, keeping the construction work firmly in the Pittsburgh family. The development adds another anchor tenant to Innovation Ridge, a business park that has become one of the North Hills' most dynamic commercial addresses.

What It Means for Pittsburgh

Armada's new headquarters arrives at a moment when Pittsburgh's major employers are collectively signaling something important: they are doubling down, not pulling back. Alongside recent office expansions from PNC, Highmark, and BNY, Armada's investment sends a clear message that the Pittsburgh region remains a destination for serious, long-term corporate commitment.

For Wexford and the North Hills corridor, the arrival of a company with Armada's scale brings an infusion of professional workers, daily foot traffic, and an institutional anchor that tends to attract further business investment. For Pittsburgh's supply chain and logistics sector, it marks a statement of confidence from one of the industry's most storied names. And for the city's broader economy, it is yet another reminder that the companies built here have a way of choosing to stay, expand, and grow right alongside the region they have called home for generations.
