Three Pittsburgh Billionaires Make Forbes World's Richest List

Edward Stack, Thomas Tull, and Maggie Hardy rank among Forbes's 2026 billionaires with combined wealth of $15.1 billion, exemplifying Pittsburgh's diverse wealth-creating leadership.

By James ColettaApril 19, 20265 min read

Pittsburgh's three billionaires built diverse enterprises spanning retail, entertainment, and consumer goods, representing the city's multifaceted economic base. // Photo: Unsplash

Three Pittsburgh residents made the 2026 Forbes World's Richest list, solidifying Pittsburgh's position among America's leading wealth-creation centers. Edward Stack, founder and owner of Dick's Sporting Goods, carries an estimated net worth of $5.4 billion. Thomas Tull, founder of Legendary Entertainment and minority owner of the Pittsburgh Steelers, holds approximately $5.4 billion in wealth. Maggie Hardy, co-owner of 84 Lumber and owner of the five-star Nemacolin resort, carries an estimated net worth of $4.3 billion. Combined, the three Pittsburgh billionaires represent $15.1 billion in total wealth, placing them among the nation's most successful entrepreneurs.

The achievement represents more than financial metrics. The three billionaires built their enterprises from Pittsburgh roots, accumulating wealth while maintaining operations and headquarters in the region. Edward Stack expanded his father's small sporting goods store into a national retail powerhouse with thousands of locations. Thomas Tull developed an entertainment production company that created blockbuster films and television productions while maintaining Pittsburgh investments including his minority Steelers stake. Maggie Hardy inherited a lumber company founded by her father and expanded it into a diversified enterprise encompassing forest products distribution and luxury hospitality. The three exemplify different paths to wealth creation within Pittsburgh's economic ecosystem.

"Pittsburgh's billionaires demonstrate that world-class entrepreneurs thrive here, building multi-billion dollar enterprises from Pittsburgh foundations while reinvesting in regional growth."

Local Business Leadership

Edward Stack built Dick's Sporting Goods from a single store into a multi-billion dollar retail enterprise. The company operates approximately 900 locations across the United States, generating over $12 billion in annual revenue. Stack's leadership transformed sporting goods retail through innovative store formats, strategic acquisitions, and supply chain optimization. The company's headquarters remain in Pittsburgh, ensuring that executive decision-making and corporate employment benefit the region directly. Stack's wealth creation demonstrates that Pittsburgh entrepreneurs can build truly massive scale enterprises competing successfully against national and international competitors.

Thomas Tull founded Legendary Entertainment as an independent film and television production company, creating major theatrical releases and streaming content. The company produced films including the Dark Knight trilogy and Inception, generating hundreds of millions in revenue. Tull's ownership of Steelers minority stake positions him as a prominent figure in Pittsburgh sports ownership, connecting entertainment industry success to regional sports community prominence. His ventures demonstrate that Pittsburgh entrepreneurs can compete in high-stakes entertainment and sports ownership without relocating to traditional media centers.

Maggie Hardy's stewardship of 84 Lumber demonstrates entrepreneurial leadership within family enterprises. The company, founded by her father Joseph Hardy, has grown into one of America's largest privately-held lumber and building materials companies. Hardy expanded operations beyond wholesale lumber into retail stores, distribution networks, and specialty products serving construction and manufacturing sectors. Her ownership of Nemacolin Woodlands Resort, a five-star destination property in western Pennsylvania, connects the family's business interests to luxury hospitality and regional tourism. Her success demonstrates that family businesses founded in Pittsburgh can generate multi-billion dollar value under subsequent-generation leadership.

The presence of three Pittsburgh billionaires on the 2026 Forbes list reflects the broader national context. The total billionaires list includes 3,428 individuals worldwide representing $20.1 trillion in aggregate wealth. Pittsburgh's three billionaires represent approximately 0.09 percent of total billionaires while controlling approximately 0.075 percent of global billionaire wealth. While statistically modest, their presence signals that Pittsburgh has created sufficient economic opportunity and entrepreneurial ecosystem to generate multiple wealth-creating enterprises capable of reaching multi-billion dollar scale.

Moving forward, Pittsburgh's three billionaires serve as visible symbols of entrepreneurial possibility for emerging founders. Their success demonstrates that world-class enterprises can originate from Pittsburgh, develop from Pittsburgh headquarters, and generate sustainable wealth. As Pittsburgh continues developing its technology, healthcare, and advanced manufacturing sectors, future generations of entrepreneurs will look to Stack, Tull, and Hardy as exemplars of what ambitious Pittsburgh builders can achieve. The city's wealth-creation capacity has never been more evident than through the success of these three billionaire leaders.
