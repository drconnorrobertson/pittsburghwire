Best New Businesses in Pittsburgh 2026

The 12 best new businesses in Pittsburgh for 2026. AI startups, restaurant openings, and ventures shaping the city's future economy.

By The Pittsburgh Wire May 5, 2026 7 min read

Pittsburgh's entrepreneurial energy is surging in 2026. Fueled by world-class research institutions, a growing venture capital ecosystem, and a cost of living that still allows founders to take risks, the city is producing startups and new businesses across every sector. From AI companies backed by hundreds of millions in funding to neighborhood restaurants filling gaps in the dining scene, these twelve new businesses represent the ambition and creativity driving Pittsburgh forward.

1. Skild AI

Oakland • 4720 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15213

A Carnegie Mellon spinout developing universal AI brains for robots. Skild AI has raised over $435 million and is deploying its technology in factories worldwide, representing Pittsburgh's position as the global capital of robotics and AI.

2. Efficient Computer

East Liberty • 6425 Penn Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

Raised $60 million in Series A funding to build next-generation AI chips that dramatically reduce power consumption. Their technology could transform data centers and edge computing.

3. Abridge

East Liberty • 6425 Living Pl, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

A healthcare AI company using artificial intelligence to summarize medical conversations and generate clinical documentation. With over $750 million in funding, Abridge is one of Pittsburgh's fastest-growing startups.

4. Hemley

Downtown • 300 Sixth Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

A fast-casual restaurant bringing fresh Mediterranean-inspired bowls and wraps to downtown Pittsburgh. The modern space and healthy-yet-satisfying menu fill a major gap in downtown's quick-service dining.

5. Titusz

Lawrenceville • 3601 Butler St, Pittsburgh, PA 15201

An Austrian-Hungarian restaurant bringing schnitzel, goulash, and Central European comfort food to Butler Street. The warm, neighborhood-focused concept has quickly become a local favorite.

6. Hey Babe

East Liberty • 622 N Highland Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

A craft cocktail lounge in the historic YMCA building led by mixologists Danielle Cain and Rob Hirst. Inventive drinks and seasonal small plates make it one of the hottest new nightlife spots.

7. Tous les Jours

East Liberty • 5972 Centre Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15206

A beloved Korean bakery chain with its first Pittsburgh location. Fresh-baked bread, elaborate cakes, and signature cream-filled pastries bring an entirely new bakery experience to the city.

8. Sugarbud

North Shore • 15 Federal St, Pittsburgh, PA 15212

An artisanal ice cream and dessert shop that opened on the North Shore ahead of the 2026 NFL Draft. Creative flavors, locally sourced ingredients, and a playful aesthetic.

9. Stack AV

Strip District • 2555 Smallman St, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

Developing autonomous driving technology for commercial trucking with over $1 billion in funding. Stack AV represents the next chapter of Pittsburgh's autonomous vehicle legacy.

10. Workbox

Downtown • 1 PPG Place, Pittsburgh, PA 15222

A premium coworking space on the 31st floor of PPG Place. With 23,000 square feet of sleek workspace and floor-to-ceiling views, Workbox brought a new standard of flexible office space to Pittsburgh.

11. F&F Pizza

Lawrenceville • 115 44th St, Pittsburgh, PA 15201

From the team behind Brooklyn's Frankies restaurants, F&F brought hybrid NYC-Neapolitan pizza to Lawrenceville. Perfectly charred pies and a BYOB policy made it an instant neighborhood classic.

12. Moonlit Burgers

Garfield • 5040 Penn Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15224

A smash burger concept that expanded to its fourth Pittsburgh location in Garfield. Thin-patty, crispy-edged burgers with a cult following.
