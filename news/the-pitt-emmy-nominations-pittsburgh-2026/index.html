Pittsburgh's ER Drama 'The Pitt' Leads All Emmy Nominations With 25 Nods

HBO Max's Pittsburgh-set medical drama swept the 2026 Emmy nominations — more than any other series — with 13 of those nods going to an ensemble cast that has become one of the most celebrated on television.

The Pittsburgh Wire
July 23, 2026
4 min read
THE PITT
The cast of HBO Max's "The Pitt," set and partially filmed in Pittsburgh. The show's second season earned 25 Emmy nominations, the most of any series in 2026. (Photo: Max)

Pittsburgh has been making headlines for its robotics startups, its revitalized riverfront, its soaring real estate investments, and its remarkable streak of national recognition. Now it can add Emmy domination to the list. HBO Max's "The Pitt," a medical drama set inside a fictional Pittsburgh trauma center, received 25 Emmy nominations this month for its second season — the most of any television series in 2026 — cementing the city's unlikely status as a cultural capital of American storytelling.

The 78th Annual Primetime Emmy Awards will air live on Monday, September 14, and Pittsburgh fans have every reason to be watching closely. The show is nominated in the most prestigious drama categories: Outstanding Drama Series, Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series for Noah Wyle as the weathered attending physician Dr. Robby, and Outstanding Supporting Actress nominations for both Katherine LaNasa and Sepideh Moafi. In one of the more remarkable achievements in Emmy history, thirteen of the 25 nominations went directly to cast members — a testament to the extraordinary ensemble the show has assembled around its Pittsburgh setting.

The show follows the staff of the Pittsburgh Trauma Medical Center through the relentless pace of a single shift — a narrative structure that has earned it comparisons to the golden era of network medical dramas while surpassing them in raw emotional honesty. Allegheny General Hospital on the North Side stands in for the fictional trauma center in exterior shots, and Pittsburghers have delighted in spotting modified signage and neighborhood Easter eggs woven into the production. The attention to Pittsburgh's specific character — the light off the Allegheny, the particular exhaustion of people who work with their hands and their hearts — has not gone unnoticed by critics.

"The Pitt has been nominated for the following Emmy categories, including outstanding drama series, outstanding lead actor, outstanding writing, and outstanding directing — a full-spectrum recognition of a show built from the ground up on Pittsburgh's story."
2026 Emmy Nominations Announcement

The show's sweep extends well beyond acting recognition. "The Pitt" received nominations for Outstanding Picture Editing for a Drama Series (twice), Outstanding Sound Mixing, Outstanding Prosthetic Makeup, Outstanding Contemporary Makeup, Outstanding Writing for a Drama Series (for both the noon and 1 p.m. episodes of the season-long day), Outstanding Directing for a Drama Series, Outstanding Production Design for a Narrative Contemporary Program, Outstanding Original Music and Lyrics, and Outstanding Casting for a Drama Series. It is, by any measure, a complete artistic achievement — and Pittsburgh is at the center of it.

A Second Season That Exceeded Its First

When "The Pitt" debuted to wide acclaim in early 2025 and subsequently earned 13 Emmy nominations — itself a strong debut — the question was whether the creative team could sustain and build on that foundation. Season 2, released in January 2026, answered that question with a resounding yes. The show's writers deepened character relationships forged in the crucible of Season 1, leaned further into the procedural rhythms of emergency medicine, and drew Pittsburgh itself more firmly into the drama. Season 3 has already been confirmed and is set to premiere in January 2027.

For Pittsburgh, the success of "The Pitt" carries meaning that transcends television. It arrives at a moment when the city is experiencing an extraordinary confluence of momentum — record venture investment, major real estate development, a robotics and AI ecosystem that has drawn global attention, and a culinary scene about to earn its first Michelin stars. A prestige television drama that celebrates Pittsburgh's working people and places its most intimate human stories in Pittsburgh's buildings, neighborhoods, and hospital corridors adds a cultural dimension to all of it. The world is not just investing in Pittsburgh. It is watching Pittsburgh, telling Pittsburgh's stories, and finding in them something worth 25 Emmy nominations.

The 78th Annual Primetime Emmy Awards air September 14 on CBS and Paramount+. "The Pitt" will compete in the drama categories against "Paradise," "Slow Horses," and "Task," among others. However the evening unfolds, the nomination count alone is already a Pittsburgh story worth celebrating.
