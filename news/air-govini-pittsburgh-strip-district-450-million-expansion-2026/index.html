Air Commits $450 Million to Pittsburgh's Strip District in Ten-Year Office Expansion

The enterprise readiness software company, formerly known as Govini, will anchor a sprawling new headquarters at Vision on Fifteenth, deepening Pittsburgh's position as a national hub for defense technology and AI-driven industry.

By Marcus Holloway July 24, 2026 4 min read

The Pittsburgh skyline, looking toward a city that continues to attract major corporate investment. // Photo: Dllu / CC BY-SA 4.0 / Wikimedia Commons

When Air — the enterprise readiness software company formerly known as Govini — took the stage at Senator David McCormick's Pennsylvania Defense and Innovation Summit last week, the room was expecting announcements. What they got was a declaration. Air pledged a ten-year, $450 million commitment to its Pittsburgh operations, anchored by a landmark new office in the Strip District that the company plans to occupy by October.

The announcement, made July 14 at the summit in Carlisle, sent a clear signal: Pittsburgh is not merely a secondary market for technology and defense software. It is, increasingly, where the industry's most consequential work gets done.

Air in Pittsburgh — By the Numbers
$450M Ten-year investment commitment to Pittsburgh operations
$10M Initial capital investment in the new Strip District office buildout
35,000 Square feet leased at Vision on Fifteenth, occupying the building's entire sixth floor
67% Workforce growth already recorded at Pittsburgh office ahead of the move

Air has quietly become one of the more significant technology tenants in the region. Founded as Govini and rebranded in recent years to reflect its broader ambitions in AI-native enterprise software, the company has built its Pittsburgh presence incrementally, floor by floor, hire by hire, in the city's booming Strip District tech corridor. The new lease encompasses the entire sixth floor of Vision on Fifteenth, a 35,000-square-foot space that will be purpose-built for the firm's growing headcount and complex systems integration work.

"Pittsburgh is where we have found the talent, the infrastructure, and the institutional partners that match our ambitions."

Air, on its Pittsburgh expansion commitment

A Bet on the Strip District's Tech Future

Vision on Fifteenth has emerged as one of the Strip District's premier addresses for knowledge-economy firms. Its proximity to the neighborhood's walkable restaurant corridor, direct access to the Allegheny riverfront, and transit connections to Oakland's university district make it a natural draw for companies competing hard for engineering and data science talent. Air's decision to anchor the building's top floor sends a message to the market about the trajectory of that corridor.

The Strip District's transformation over the past decade has been remarkable even by Pittsburgh standards. What was once a working waterfront of meat packers, produce wholesalers, and industrial suppliers has evolved into a dense cluster of robotics firms, AI companies, and defense technology contractors, all within walking distance of each other. Air's expansion adds another prominent name to a roster that already includes some of the region's most innovative technology employers.

The Defense Tech Dividend

Air's growth in Pittsburgh is part of a broader surge in defense-adjacent technology investment that has accelerated across western Pennsylvania since 2024. The Pennsylvania Defense and Innovation Summit, where the announcement was made, convened more than a dozen companies that together committed nearly $10 billion in new investment and more than 4,000 Pennsylvania jobs in two days. Air's $450 million pledge was among the most substantial single-company commitments in that group.

The company specializes in what it calls enterprise readiness — AI-native systems that help defense organizations move from information to action faster and more reliably than traditional software platforms allow. It is a growing category, and Pittsburgh, with its deep bench of computer science graduates from Carnegie Mellon University and the University of Pittsburgh, has proven to be fertile ground for the talent pipelines that sustain it.

For the city, the practical impact of a commitment of this scale is measured not only in office square footage but in the ripple effects that follow. Each high-wage technology job created by an anchor employer typically supports several additional service and support roles across the regional economy. With Air's Pittsburgh workforce already up 67 percent in recent years, the acceleration that comes with a dedicated, purpose-built space could be substantial.

The new office is expected to be ready for occupancy in October, setting up a fall arrival that coincides with what promises to be a strong hiring season for technology firms across the city. For the Strip District — and for Pittsburgh at large — it is one more piece of evidence that the city's long-running story of reinvention is far from finished.
