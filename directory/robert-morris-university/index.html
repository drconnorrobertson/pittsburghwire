The Story

Robert Morris University was founded in 1921 and is located on a 230-acre campus in Moon Township, just west of Pittsburgh. The university serves over 4,500 students across undergraduate and graduate programs, with particular strengths in business, engineering, nursing, and cybersecurity.

RMU has positioned itself as one of Pittsburgh's most career-focused universities, with strong employer partnerships and co-op programs that connect students directly to the regional job market. The university's School of Engineering and the School of Nursing are major pipelines for talent entering Pittsburgh's healthcare and technology sectors.

The UPMC Events Center on RMU's campus has become a regional entertainment and sports venue, hosting concerts, conferences, and collegiate athletics. RMU's Division I athletics program, the Colonials, competes in the Horizon League and adds to Pittsburgh's rich sports culture.

What Makes It Pittsburgh

Robert Morris University represents the Pittsburgh region's commitment to accessible, career-focused higher education. Named after a Founding Father and financier of the American Revolution, RMU has educated generations of Pittsburgh professionals who stay in the region and build careers.

RMU's partnerships with major Pittsburgh employers including UPMC, PNC, and regional engineering firms create a talent pipeline that keeps skilled graduates in the city. The university is a key part of Pittsburgh's strategy for workforce development and economic growth.
