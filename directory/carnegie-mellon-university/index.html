The Story

Carnegie Mellon University was founded in 1900 by Andrew Carnegie, the legendary industrialist whose steel fortune transformed Pittsburgh. What began as a technical school evolved into one of the world's leading research institutions, consistently ranked among the top universities globally.

Today, CMU is home to some of the most prestigious programs in computer science, engineering, and artificial intelligence. The university's School of Computer Science is regularly ranked number one in the nation, while the Robotics Institute pioneered autonomous vehicle technology that shaped an entire industry. From self-driving cars to machine learning breakthroughs, CMU research drives innovation across the world.

With 16,000 students and over 5,000 employees, Carnegie Mellon is one of Pittsburgh's largest institutional anchors. The university attracts world-class talent from every continent, yet remains rooted in Pittsburgh. Under the leadership of President Farnam Jahanian, CMU continues expanding its influence in AI, robotics, and computational thinking, positioning itself at the forefront of 21st-century innovation.

What Makes It Pittsburgh

Carnegie Mellon embodies Pittsburgh's reinvention. Founded by a steel baron at the height of the Industrial Era, CMU has transformed from an industrial trade school into an innovation engine that powers Pittsburgh's knowledge economy. The university represents the city's ability to leverage historic wealth and resources to build institutions that shape the future.

The Robotics Institute alone has produced startups, patents, and talent that have reshaped autonomous transportation, manufacturing, and AI research globally. Many of Pittsburgh's most successful tech companies, from Duolingo to Aurora Innovation, were born from CMU research and talent pipelines. This deep connection between academic innovation and commercial success is uniquely Pittsburgh.
