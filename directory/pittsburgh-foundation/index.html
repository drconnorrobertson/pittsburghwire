The Story

The Pittsburgh Foundation was established in 1945 as one of America's oldest and most influential community foundations. With over 1.4 billion in assets, the foundation has granted more than 850 million dollars to regional nonprofits since its founding, making it a defining force in Pittsburgh's civic landscape.

The foundation's mission centers on advancing equity, education, and community development across the Pittsburgh region. By bringing together philanthropic resources from individuals, families, and corporations, the foundation has become a trusted steward of charitable giving, directing resources to the most critical needs in the community. Its grants have supported education initiatives, healthcare programs, community development projects, and racial equity work.

Under President and CEO Lisa Schroeder's leadership, the foundation continues to evolve its grantmaking strategies to address systemic challenges and support grassroots organizations. With 50 staff members and a robust community of donors, the Pittsburgh Foundation remains a vital institution connecting philanthropy to community needs and aspirations.

What Makes It Pittsburgh

The Pittsburgh Foundation is deeply rooted in the city's philanthropic tradition. Born during the post-World War II boom, the foundation reflects Pittsburgh's history of civic leadership and industrial wealth channeled into social good. The foundation's work demonstrates Pittsburgh's commitment to supporting neighbors and building stronger communities.

From supporting the Cultural District to funding workforce development, the foundation's grants are woven into Pittsburgh's social fabric. The foundation's focus on equity and community voice reflects modern Pittsburgh values, positioning the city as a leader in values-driven philanthropy focused on systemic change rather than charity alone.
