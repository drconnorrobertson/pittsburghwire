The Story

Abridge was founded in 2018 by Dr. Shiv Rao with a simple but powerful insight: doctors spend too much time on documentation and not enough time with patients. The company's solution is an AI-powered platform that listens to conversations between doctors and patients, then automatically generates clinical documentation.

The technology uses advanced natural language processing to understand medical conversations, extract relevant information, and summarize it in the format clinicians need. The impact is immediate: doctors regain time to focus on patient care rather than paperwork.

Abridge has grown rapidly, raising over $150 million in funding and establishing partnerships with major health systems including UPMC, one of Pittsburgh's largest employers. The company employs hundreds of people in Oakland and has become a flagship example of healthcare innovation emerging from Pittsburgh.

What Makes It Pittsburgh

Pittsburgh's healthcare ecosystem—anchored by UPMC, Allegheny Health Network, and academic medical centers—creates a natural testing ground for healthcare innovation. Abridge leverages that advantage, working closely with local health systems to refine technology that eventually scales nationally.

The company represents a new Pittsburgh economy: one where healthcare, technology, and innovation intersect. Rather than Pittsburgh being a place where healthcare IT is imported from elsewhere, companies like Abridge are developing and exporting it to the world.
